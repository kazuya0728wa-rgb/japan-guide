🗾 Japan Travel Plan PREMIUM

TOKYO

Flexible 1-day food & sightseeing plan
Best for: First-time visitors, couples, friends, food + iconic photo spots.
☀️ Morning
09:00 ⚠️ Popular
Tsukiji Outer Market
⏱ ~90 min 💴 ¥800–1,500 Free entry

Seafood breakfast, market walk, casual food stalls. Good start — many shops open early and items sell out by mid-morning.

💡 Pro Tip: Most stalls open by 6–7am. Popular items like fresh sushi and tamagoyaki sell out before 10:30am. Go straight to Marutake for tamagoyaki. The "inner market" moved to Toyosu — the outer market here is the food stall area.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Heavy rain: Go directly to Ginza underground cafes or Tokyo Station basement (Gransta / Ramen Street / Ichibangai).
Very crowded: Arrive at 08:00 instead — the market is best before tour groups arrive after 10am.
🚶 Walk 3–8 min within Tsukiji area · same neighborhood
09:25
Tsukiji Yamacho / Sushizanmai
⏱ ~40 min 💴 ¥600–1,500

Tamagoyaki snack at Yamacho OR sit-down sushi brunch at Sushizanmai. Keep it light — lunch and snacks come later in the day.

💡 Pro Tip: Tsukiji Yamacho is the most-photographed tamagoyaki shop — buy one to eat on the go (¥150–200). Sushizanmai is open 24h with English menus, great for first-timers who want to sit.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Long line: Skip individual shops and treat the whole Tsukiji area as a food-walk. Many small vendors along the main street are equally good.
Rain: Sushizanmai has a full indoor restaurant — ideal rain shelter.
🚇 Tsukiji → Asakusa: Toei Oedo Line or Toei Asakusa Line · ~30–35 min · ¥180–210
🚕 Taxi option: ~¥1,500–2,000 (25 min, comfortable with luggage)
11:00 🔴 Very popular
Sensoji Temple + Nakamise Street
⏱ ~2 h 💴 ¥0–1,000 Temple: Free

Classic temple, old Tokyo atmosphere, snacks and souvenirs on Nakamise. The Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate) is the iconic photo spot at the entrance.

💡 Pro Tip: The temple itself is open 24/7, but the inner chamber closes around 17:00–18:00. For photos without crowds, aim to arrive before noon or after 18:00. Try ningyo-yaki (small cakes) on Nakamise for ¥200–400.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Too crowded at peak: Walk 10 min east to Senso-ji side streets (Hoppy Street / Shin-Nakamise) — much calmer with local food and bars.
Alternative day: Visit Ueno Park and Tokyo National Museum (¥1,000, great indoor option). Ueno station is 15 min from Asakusa.
🌤️ Afternoon
🚇 Asakusa → Harajuku: Tokyo Metro Ginza Line to Shibuya (22 min), then 1 min walk to Harajuku area · ~35–45 min total · ¥210
14:00
Meiji Jingu + Takeshita Street
⏱ ~2 h 💴 ¥500–1,500 Shrine: Free

Walk through the quiet forest to the main shrine, then switch gears to Harajuku's youth culture — sweets, crepes, unique fashion on Takeshita Street (350m).

💡 Pro Tip: The forest walk to the shrine is 10 min each way — peaceful and completely free. Takeshita Street starts right at Harajuku station East exit. Marion Crepes is the most popular crepe shop (¥500–700).
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Rain: Skip the shrine forest and go directly to Harajuku/Omotesando area — both have covered shopping (Laforet, Omotesando Hills mall).
Alternative: Omotesando avenue is the high-end street just south — great window shopping and architecturally interesting.
🚶 Walk 25 min · OR 🚃 JR Yamanote Line 1 stop (3 min, ¥150) · Harajuku → Shibuya
🌆 Evening
16:30 📅 Book SKY in advance
Shibuya Crossing + optional SHIBUYA SKY
⏱ ~2 h 💴 ¥0 + ¥2,000 SKY

Iconic scramble crossing and Hachiko statue (free). If booked in advance, SHIBUYA SKY rooftop gives a dramatic sunset city view at 229m.

💡 Pro Tip: Crossing is most impressive at rush hour (18:00–19:00). Best photo angle: 2F Starbucks window (Q-FRONT building) — arrive 30 min early to grab a window seat. SHIBUYA SKY: book online (save ~¥500 vs. door price) and pick a sunset slot (17:00–18:30).
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Shibuya too crowded: Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building (Tocho) in Shinjuku has a free observation deck (202m). ~20 min by train from Shibuya.
Rainy + no SKY: Explore Shibuya Hikarie or PARCO shopping complex — both excellent indoor options.
🚶 Walk / train 5–20 min depending on choice · Shibuya / Omotesando area
19:00
ICHIRAN Shibuya or Tonkatsu Maisen Aoyama
⏱ ~60–75 min 💴 ¥1,200–2,500

ICHIRAN: individual booth ramen — simple ordering, good for solo travelers. Maisen: classic tonkatsu in a historic Taisho-era bathhouse building, perfect after Omotesando.

💡 Pro Tip: ICHIRAN uses a ticket machine (English options available). Arriving at 19:00 hits the sweet spot — busy but before the post-theater peak. Maisen Aoyama closes around 21:00 on weekdays; check hours. Reservations not possible at ICHIRAN — just queue.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Long line at both: Shibuya/Omotesando department store restaurant floors (B1 or top floors of Shibuya Hikarie, Shibuya Scramble Square) have reliable options with shorter waits.

⚡ Flexible Alternatives
🌧️ Rainy Day
Switch to: Tokyo National Museum → Ginza underground / Tokyo Station food hall → teamLab Planets (book in advance).
Reason: More indoor time, less walking outside. Tokyo Station's underground concourse connects many dry areas. teamLab Planets is completely indoor and one of the most memorable experiences in Japan.
😤 Very Crowded Asakusa
Switch to: Visit Sensoji very early morning or evening instead. Replace the midday slot with Ueno Park + Ueno Zoo (¥600) or Ginza area.
Reason: Asakusa and Nakamise become extremely stressful 11:00–15:00, especially on weekends and holidays. Early morning (before 09:00) is magical — almost no one there.
😴 Too Tired
Switch to: Tsukiji → Ginza walk → Tokyo Station (shopping + lunch) → Shinjuku free view (Tocho) only.
Reason: Shorter total walking distance. Ginza and Tokyo Station have excellent rest spots, cafes, and indoor shopping. Tocho free observation deck is a good alternative to SHIBUYA SKY.
🍜 Food-First Traveler
Switch to: Tsukiji (extended 2h food tour) → Asakusa snacks (ningyo-yaki, senbei) → Harajuku sweets (crepes, Laduree) → Shibuya ramen dinner.
Reason: Turns sightseeing into a food route. Each area has distinct food identity. You still see Tokyo's key neighborhoods while eating local at every stop.
💰 Low Budget
Switch to: Tsukiji snacks (¥500) → Free Sensoji → Free Meiji Jingu → Shibuya Crossing (free) → Tocho free view → convenience store dinner.
Reason: Reduces paid tickets to near zero while keeping all iconic spots. 7-Eleven/Lawson dinner ¥500–700 is genuinely good in Japan. Total day budget: ¥1,500–2,500.

KYOTO

Flexible 1-day culture, temples & food plan
Best for: First-time Kyoto visitors, culture lovers, couples, food + old-street photos.
☀️ Morning
08:00 🔴 Very popular Free · 24h
Fushimi Inari Taisha
⏱ 1.5–2 h 💴 Free

Start early for the famous red torii gates. Walk only as far as your energy allows — the first 30 min of trail (to Yotsutsuji viewpoint) is the most photogenic section.

💡 Pro Tip: At 08:00 the tunnel of torii has only a handful of people — by 10:00 it's packed with tour groups. Full summit hike (Inari-san) takes 2–3 hours round trip. The Yotsutsuji viewpoint is about 30 min up and gives you a great city view + near-empty torii shots. Kyoto Station to JR Inari: JR Nara Line, 5 min, ¥140.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Crowded: Come before 08:00 or after 16:00 — the torii look beautiful at dusk too. Check the Kyoto City congestion forecast on holidays.
Rain: The torii tunnels are covered and atmospheric in rain — actually a great photo opportunity. Bring waterproof shoes as the stone paths get slippery.
🚌 Fushimi Inari → Kiyomizu-dera: Walk 10 min to Tofukuji, then Bus 207 to Kiyomizumichi (~30 min, ¥230) · OR taxi ¥1,200–1,500 (~20 min) · Total: ~40–50 min
10:15 🔴 Very popular 💴 ¥500 entry
Kiyomizu-dera Temple + Ninenzaka / Sannenzaka
⏱ ~2 h 💴 ¥500–1,500

Classic Kyoto temple view from the wooden stage (no nails used). Then walk down through beautifully preserved Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka streets — souvenir shops and cafes.

💡 Pro Tip: Buy your temple ticket at the main Niomon gate (¥500, no advance booking needed). The wooden stage photo is best from the Okuno-in sub-temple to the left. Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka below takes about 20–30 min to walk and has many matcha soft cream shops (¥400–600).
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Streets packed: Shorten time on Sannenzaka and head directly to Gion by taxi (~¥1,000) or bus.
Note on repairs: Some parts of the Kiyomizu complex have ongoing restoration work — the main stage and temple are fully accessible. Check the official site for current status.
🌤️ Afternoon
🚶 Walk ~20–30 min (scenic route through Sannenzaka and Gion area) · OR 🚌 Bus 207 to Shijo (~15 min, ¥230)
12:45 ⚠️ Popular at lunch
Nishiki Market
⏱ ~60–75 min 💴 ¥500–1,200

Kyoto's "kitchen" — 400m covered arcade with Kyoto snacks, yuba (tofu skin), pickles, matcha sweets. Eat at shop counters — walking while eating is considered rude here.

💡 Pro Tip: Best stops — Yubahan (yuba tofu), Nishiki Warai (grilled octopus skewer), Murakami-ju (Kyoto pickles), Nishiki Wakana (matcha ice cream). Market shops generally open 10:00–18:00. Very narrow at peak lunch — go to one end and work through systematically.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Too crowded: Visit after 15:00 instead (fewer tour groups). Alternative: eat lunch at a restaurant in the Kawaramachi/Shijo area nearby.
Note: Several Nishiki Market shops close on Wednesdays.
🚶 Walk ~8–12 min to Honke Owariya · OR ~15 min walk to Gion Izuju
14:30
Honke Owariya or Gion Izuju
⏱ ~60–75 min 💴 ¥1,200–1,800

Honke Owariya: historic soba restaurant operating since 1465 — a genuine piece of Kyoto history. Gion Izuju: Kyoto-style pressed sushi (battera) — very local taste.

💡 Pro Tip: Honke Owariya is on Teramachi-dori and opens for lunch at 11:00. The hiyashi soba (cold soba) is recommended in summer. Gion Izuju's saba battera (mackerel pressed sushi) is a Kyoto specialty you won't find easily outside Kansai.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Lines too long: Kyoto Station has reliable, good-quality restaurants underground (The Cube food hall) — no reservations needed, and it's on your route home. Budget ¥1,000–1,500.
🌆 Evening
🚶 Walk ~15–25 min to Gion / Hanamikoji area · OR 🚌 Bus to Gion-Shijo (~10 min, ¥230)
16:00
Gion + Kiyomizu area / Pontocho walk
⏱ ~2 h 💴 Free walking

Evening walk through historic streets — Hanamikoji Street, Shimbashi area (lined with traditional ochaya teahouses), then riverside Pontocho alley with its intimate lantern-lit restaurants.

💡 Pro Tip: Best time to spot geiko/maiko is 17:30–18:30 near Hanamikoji Street — they walk to appointments. No touching, no flash, keep distance. Pontocho riverside (Kamo River) terraces open in summer (May–Sep) — magical dinner experience. Yasaka Shrine at the east end is beautiful at dusk (free, always open).
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Rain: Switch to Kyoto International Manga Museum (¥900, near Karasuma Oike station) or Kyoto Railway Museum (¥1,200, near Kyoto Station) — both excellent indoor options.
Alternative evening: Nijo Castle illumination events (seasonal, check schedule).
🚶 Walk ~20–30 min to Menbaka · OR 🚌 Bus to Kyoto Station area
18:30 📅 Reservation strongly recommended
Menbaka Fire Ramen or Pontocho dinner
⏱ ~60–75 min 💴 ¥1,200–2,000

Menbaka: theatrical ramen where burning scallion oil is poured on your bowl tableside — dramatic and memorable. Or a more atmospheric Pontocho dinner with river views.

💡 Pro Tip: Menbaka opens for dinner around 17:30 and has limited seats — call ahead or use the reservation system on their website. The "fire" only happens if you add the scallion oil — staff will warn you. Very popular for content creators. Pontocho alternative: look for restaurants showing plastic food models outside with English menus — these are tourist-friendly.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Menbaka fully booked: Ramen near Kyoto Station (Kyoto Ramen Koji on the 10th floor) or return to Pontocho for a sit-down dinner. Pontocho also has some accessible casual spots for ¥1,500–2,500.

Experience Ramen
Main:Menbaka Fire Ramen

Backup 2:Pontocho dinner (any accessible restaurant)

⚡ Flexible Alternatives
🌧️ Rainy Day
Switch to: Kyoto Station → Nishiki Market → Kyoto International Manga Museum → Menbaka Fire Ramen
Reason: More indoor or covered stops; fewer temple stairs. Kyoto Station itself is architecturally impressive and worth exploring on a rainy day.
😤 Crowded Fushimi / Kiyomizu
Switch to: Use Kyoto City congestion map and shift Fushimi to 07:00 (less crowded) or late afternoon 16:00+.
Reason: Kyoto crowds can completely change the experience. Fushimi at 08:00 vs. 11:00 feels like a different place. Kiyomizu is best visited on weekday mornings.
😴 Too Tired
Switch to: Kyoto Station → Nishiki Market → Gion / Pontocho evening walk only.
Reason: Shorter transfers and still feels authentically like Kyoto. Gion in the evening is the most atmospheric part of the city and requires no uphill walking.
🌿 Nature-First
Switch to: Replace the downtown afternoon with Arashiyama (bamboo grove, Togetsukyo bridge, Tenryuji temple garden).
Reason: Better for bamboo grove, river scenery, and relaxed photos. Arashiyama from Kyoto Station: Sagano Line, ~15 min, ¥240.
👨‍👩‍👧 Family / Kids
Switch to: Kyoto Station → Kyoto Railway Museum (¥1,200 adult / ¥500 child) → Nishiki Market → easy dinner.
Reason: Less walking, more indoor learning and rest. Kids love the train simulators. The museum is right next to Kyoto Station.

⚠️ Kyoto Manners: Do not block narrow streets for photos — locals walk these daily. At Nishiki Market, eat at the shop or in designated areas — do not walk and eat. Geiko/maiko should be observed respectfully from a distance, never touched or blocked for selfies.

OSAKA

Flexible 1-day food, castle & nightlife plan
Best for: Food lovers, friends, first-time Osaka visitors, night-photo travelers.
☀️ Morning
09:30 💴 ¥600 tower
Osaka Castle Museum
⏱ ~90 min Park: Free

Castle exterior, park, and museum if interested in history. Opens 09:00. The park grounds are free — tower entry is optional (¥600 with elevator access).

💡 Pro Tip: The castle tower closes at 17:00 (last entry 16:30) — visit early so you have the option. Best exterior photo: from the South bridge or the Nishi-no-Maru garden. The museum inside covers Toyotomi Hideyoshi's era with English panels.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Rain: Spend more time inside the museum. The tower has good views even in light rain.
Start later option: If you prefer sleeping in, shift to 10:30 start and reduce castle time to 60 min — still time for everything.
🚇 Osaka Castle → Kuromon: Osaka Metro Nagahori Tsurumi-ryokuchi Line · Tanimachi Yonchome → Nipponbashi (2 stops, 5 min, ¥230) · Walk 5 min to Kuromon · Total ~20 min
🌤️ Afternoon
11:30 ⚠️ Popular at lunch
Kuromon Market
⏱ ~90 min 💴 ¥500–1,500

Osaka's kitchen — 170+ stalls with fresh seafood, grilled scallops, wagyu beef, fruit. Eat as you go. The market is about 580m long — one pass is enough.

💡 Pro Tip: Must-try: fresh sea urchin (uni) on toast (~¥500), grilled scallops (~¥300), wagyu skewer (~¥600–1,000). Most shops open 08:00–18:00 — afternoon is fresher than morning for cooked items. Bring cash — not all stalls accept cards.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Too crowded: Move directly to Namba area restaurants and return to Kuromon at 14:00–15:00 when the lunch rush subsides.
All closed (check holidays): Kuromon has some closures on Sundays for individual shops. Namba restaurants nearby are always available.
🚶 Walk ~10–15 min · Kuromon to Namba / Dotonbori area
13:30
Namba / Dotonbori walk
⏱ ~30 min 💴 Free walking

Shopping, photos, short cafe break. Shinsaibashi-suji covered arcade is just north of Dotonbori — 600m of shops. Good time to explore before lunch crowds thin.

💡 Pro Tip: The Dotonbori Canal cruise (¥900, 20 min) departs from Namba Walk pier — unique perspective on the neon signs. Den-Den Town (electronics/anime) is 10 min east for otaku shopping. Covered Shinsaibashi arcade connects north toward Umeda underground.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Rain: Use the extensive covered arcades (Shinsaibashi-suji, America-mura, Namba Walk underground) — you can walk for hours without getting wet.
🚶 Walk 5–10 min within Dotonbori area
14:00 ⚠️ Lines possible
Okonomiyaki Mizuno or Dotonbori Imai
⏱ ~60–75 min 💴 ¥1,200–1,600

Mizuno: Osaka-style okonomiyaki (yamaimoiri type with mountain yam — fluffy and light). Imai: classic udon in a calmer, local-feeling setting if okonomiyaki isn't your preference.

💡 Pro Tip: Mizuno opens at 11:00. The yamaimo-iri pork okonomiyaki is the signature dish (~¥1,200). The cook grills it on the table in front of you. Arriving at 14:00 is better than 12:00 — the lunch rush subsides. Imai has been serving udon since 1949 and is in a beautiful old building.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Mizuno line too long: Chibo (nearby) or any other Dotonbori okonomiyaki restaurant — there are many options within 2 minutes' walk.
Prefer something lighter: Kani Doraku (crab restaurant) or any sushi counter along Dotonbori are excellent alternatives.
🌆 Evening
🚶 Walk 5 min within Dotonbori · Glico sign is at Ebisubashi bridge
16:00
Dotonbori + Takoya Dotonbori Kukuru
⏱ ~90 min 💴 ¥500–800

Glico running man sign, canal walk, takoyaki from Kukuru (melting center, 8 pieces). This is the classic Osaka street-food mood — neon signs, crowds, energy.

💡 Pro Tip: The Glico sign is best photographed from Ebisubashi bridge at dusk (around 18:00–18:30) when it lights up against the fading sky. Kukuru's takoyaki have a liquid center — let them cool slightly before eating. The canal cruise (¥900) from Namba Walk pier is especially atmospheric in the evening.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Too crowded: Take photos from side streets or from the bridge. Come back at 21:00–22:00 for night photos with fewer people blocking the shot.
Alternative snack: Rikuro Ojisan cheesecake shop is nearby — the fresh-baked jiggly cheesecake (~¥935 for whole cake) is a famous Osaka souvenir.
🚇 Osaka Metro Midosuji Line · Namba → Dobutsuen-mae (2 stops, 4 min, ¥230) · Walk 5 min to Shinsekai · Total ~20 min
18:00
Shinsekai + Kushikatsu Daruma
⏱ ~90 min 💴 ¥1,500–2,000

Retro Osaka at its most authentic — neon-lit showa-era streets around Tsutenkaku tower. Daruma is the legendary kushikatsu (breaded fried skewers) restaurant.

💡 Pro Tip: The #1 Kushikatsu Rule: NEVER dip twice into the shared sauce — pour it over, or the staff will stop you. Order one by one and mix different types. Daruma has multiple branches in Shinsekai — the original is the smaller one. Arrive at 18:00–18:30 to avoid the worst wait. Budget: ~¥1,500–2,000 for 10–15 skewers + drinks.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Tired / no Shinsekai: Stay in Dotonbori and find a izakaya or sushi restaurant. The Dotonbori area has everything within walking distance.
Rain: Shinsekai has some covered arcades — still doable in light rain. Heavy rain, skip and head to Umeda underground.
🚇 Shinsekai → Umeda: Osaka Metro Yotsubashi Line · Daikokucho → Namba → Hommachi → Nishi-Umeda (~20 min, ¥280) · Walk 10 min to Umeda Sky Building · Total ~30–35 min
20:00 💴 ¥1,500
Umeda Sky Building or Dotonbori night
⏱ ~60 min 💴 ¥1,500 + transport

Night view from the open-air 40th floor (last entry 22:00) — 360° rooftop with Osaka city lights. Or stay in Dotonbori for neon nightlife photos instead.

💡 Pro Tip: Umeda Sky Building opens until 22:30 (last entry 22:00). The rooftop is open-air — bring a light jacket. The connecting escalator tunnel between the two towers is architecturally unique. Arriving at 20:00 gives you the full nighttime view. The Dotonbori alternative at night has incredible neon reflections on the canal — best around 21:00 when the photo crowds thin out.
⚡ Backup if crowded / rainy
Bad weather: Umeda underground city (Daimaru, Lucua) and Hep Five Ferris wheel (¥600, indoor ride at 106m) are covered alternatives.
Too tired: Skip Umeda entirely and enjoy Dotonbori night photos instead — free and very photogenic.

⚡ Flexible Alternatives
🌧️ Rainy Day
Switch to: Osaka Castle Museum (indoor) → Kuromon Market (partially covered) → Namba covered arcades → Umeda underground city / Umeda Sky Building
Reason: Keeps most of the day indoors or under cover. Umeda underground is one of the largest underground shopping areas in Japan — dry and extensive.
😤 Very Crowded Dotonbori
Switch to: Take Glico sign photos from the bridge at 16:30 (pre-dinner, fewer people) OR go back at 21:30–22:00 (post-dinner, calmer).
Reason: Crowds and restaurant lines peak 17:00–20:00 around Dotonbori. Eat before 17:00 or after 20:30 for noticeably shorter waits.
😴 Too Tired
Switch to: Kuromon Market → Namba → Dotonbori only.
Reason: This trio has the best food density with minimal transport. All three are within 15 min walk of each other. Skip Osaka Castle and Shinsekai for a more relaxed pace.
🍜 Food-First Traveler
Switch to: Kuromon → Mizuno → Kukuru → Rikuro → Daruma — a complete Osaka food crawl.
Reason: Turns the entire day into a curated food tour. This route covers market food, the two iconic Osaka dishes (okonomiyaki + kushikatsu), takoyaki, and dessert. Skip sightseeing entirely and just eat.
👨‍👩‍👧 Family / Rainy Alternative
Switch to: Add Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan (¥2,400 adults / ¥1,200 children, 2–3h) and reduce nightlife stops.
Reason: One of the world's best aquariums — whale shark tank is the centerpiece. Near Osaka Castle by metro (~30 min). Reduce Shinsekai/Umeda to fit it in.
